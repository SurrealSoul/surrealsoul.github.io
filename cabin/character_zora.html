The Fortune Teller

A "spiritualist" Cornelius hired for "entertainment." She's been predicting doom since she arrived.

Her Secret

She's a complete fraud. She's also Beatrice's (the sister's) paid accomplice, hired to "spook" Cornelius into changing his will in Beatrice's favor.

Background

Madam Zora (real name: Zelda Murphy) is a carnival fortune teller who has spent years perfecting the art of cold reading and psychological manipulation. She travels from town to town, setting up her crystal ball and tarot cards to fleece gullible clients. Her theatrical appearance and mysterious persona are carefully crafted to hide her true nature as a con artist.

Motive

Beatrice Crimson hired Madam Zora to psychologically manipulate Cornelius into changing his will in her favor. The plan was for Zora to fill Cornelius with dread about his mortality and guilt about not taking care of his "dear sister." However, when Cornelius saw through the charade and threatened to expose both women, Zora realized she needed to take more drastic action to protect herself.

Opportunity

As the hired entertainment, Madam Zora had access to most areas of the cabin and was able to observe everyone's behavior. Her knowledge of psychological manipulation and her partnership with Beatrice (who has expertise with poisons) could have made them a deadly team. She also had private sessions with several guests, gathering information that could be useful.

Alibi

Claims she was meditating in the conservatory. She says the spirits were particularly restless that night.
