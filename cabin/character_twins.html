The Chef's Children

Chef Antoine's silent, creepy 12-year-old twins. They just watch... and whisper to each other.

Their Secret

They stole the master key from the butler's office. They've been all over the cabin and... they say they saw the killer.

Background

Lily and Leo Dubois are Chef Antoine's precocious and unsettling 12-year-old twins. Homeschooled and rarely exposed to other children, they've developed an almost telepathic connection and communicate primarily through whispers and meaningful looks. Their father brings them to his catering jobs because he has nowhere else to leave them, and they've learned to entertain themselves by exploring and observing the adult world around them.

Motive

The twins don't appear to have a direct motive to kill Cornelius, but their access to the master key and their tendency to wander the cabin unseen makes them perfect witnesses - or accomplices. Their loyalty to their father might drive them to help cover up his crimes, or their childish sense of justice might motivate them to take action against someone they perceive as having wronged their family.

Opportunity

The twins stole the master key from Arthur's office, giving them access to every room in the cabin. Their small size and quiet nature allow them to move through the building unnoticed, and their habit of exploring means they've likely discovered secret passages and hiding spots that adults don't know about. They also have access to their father's kitchen and all its potential weapons.

Alibi

Claim they were asleep in their room. However, Genevieve claims she saw them wandering the halls holding hands.
